What fits on an Agilex 3 100K — and how fast it classifies from HyperRAM

Every number in this document reduces to one question: does the workload hit the compute wall (2,760 INT8 MACs/cycle in tensor mode) or the memory wall (an 8-bit HyperBus at a few hundred MB/s)? The dividing line is ~350–400 KB of INT8 weights — the point where a model stops fitting in on-chip SRAM.

§2 · Compute ceiling

Where 1.90 TOPS comes from, and what survives contact with reality

The marketing number decomposes exactly: 138 DSP × 20 INT8 MACs × 2 ops × 345 MHz = 1,904 GOPS ≈ 1.90 TOPS. It closes to three significant figures, which tells you it assumes every DSP in tensor mode, 100% issue efficiency, at maximum fabric clock. None of those hold in a real design, so derate in three stages:

  • Fabric clock. A packed 100K-LE design with an accelerator, DMA, Nios V, and a HyperBus controller will not close at 345 MHz on the first pass. Plan 250–300 MHz.
  • MAC allocation. The inference IP will not get all 138 DSPs — the rest of the system (DMA, address generators, any classic-mode DSP use) takes a cut. Plan 70–85%.
  • Scheduler efficiency. Fraction of allocated MAC-cycles doing useful work. Layer-edge drain/fill, depthwise layers that map poorly to tensor mode, and buffer stalls land sequenced overlays at 30–60% on CNNs. Small models sit at the low end because fixed per-inference overhead dominates.

§3 · Performance levers, Agilex 3-specific

Seven disciplines that separate the datasheet from a shipped fmax

Each lever names the mistake that costs performance, the discipline that recovers it, and the §7 ladder test that proves the recovery with a number. This list is the specialization: generic "FPGA optimization" advice ends where these begin.

LV 1

Retiming-clean RTL — 2nd-gen Hyperflex

Agilex puts registers in the routing fabric, and Hyper-Retiming can only use them where the RTL permits. Discipline: reset-less pipeline registers on the datapath (synchronous reset only where the architecture truly needs state), no clock gating, duplicate high-fanout control, and treat the Fast Forward report as a work queue rather than commentary. Proof → L1: the same PE array compiled reset-heavy vs retiming-clean; the fmax delta is the cash value of the discipline. Moves the fabric-clock slider.

LV 2

Tensor-mode capture

The 20-INT8 mode is a structured block — two tensor columns with INT32/FP32 cascade accumulation and a block-floating exponent — and naive a*b+c inference does not find it; it lands in classic 18×19 mode at 2 multipliers per block, a silent 10×. Discipline: instantiate or use the DSP UG templates, shape the dataflow to the block’s native dot-product, cascade accumulation through the DSP column instead of ALM adder trees, and audit the fitter report for tensor-mode block count on every build. Proof → L0. Moves the allocation and scheduler sliders.

LV 3

M20K banking geometry

The ~330 GB/s aggregate figure exists only if weights and activations are banked one-port-per-PE-column, output registers enabled in the hot domain, widths matched to lane count. A single shared port is a serialization you will not see until the scheduler stalls. Proof → L2 measures achieved GB/s per banking configuration. Converts the on-chip “unlimited” claim into a number.

LV 4

Clock-domain split

Hot domain for the PE array and its M20K banks; cool domain (100–150 MHz) for Nios V, CSRs, and housekeeping; HyperBus in its own. Async FIFOs at the seams. One merged domain lets a lazy CSR decode set the ceiling for 138 DSPs. Proof → L1, merged vs isolated compile of the identical array. Moves the fabric-clock slider.

LV 5

Soft-logic MAC overflow

Past 138 DSPs, sub-8-bit MACs go into ALMs, where Quartus fractal synthesis optimizes soft multiplier trees (confirm Agilex 3 family support in the current synthesis handbook). This is what makes INT4 and ternary nets scale past the DSP count. Proof → L0b: MACs per kALM and fmax at INT4/INT2/INT1. Extends the amber ceiling for aggressively quantized models.

LV 6

Trained HyperBus capture

RWDS-strobed reads want constraints referenced to RWDS and an init-time training pass that centers the capture window per board and temperature — static constraints alone leave margin (and MHz) on the table. Proof → L3 shmoo: HyperBus clock vs error rate, then sustained MB/s at the chosen operating point. Replaces both cyan sliders with measurements.

LV 7

Quantization aggression, priced by the harness

Each bit removed multiplies effective compute only where the datapath exploits it (LV5, packing), and costs accuracy the §6 harness measures on real records. The output is an accuracy-vs-bits Pareto per model — the signature plot of the whole exercise, and the thing generic benchmarks never publish. Proof → L5.

§4 · Memory walls

Three tiers, three very different bandwidths

TierCapacityPeak BWSustained (plan)Role in this design

400 MB/s @200 MHz DDR		 ~250 MB/s Test-record store (§6); weight store for models >~400 KB
QSPI flash32 MB~50–66 MB/s (×4, 100–133 MHz)~40–55 MB/s Bitstream + full-dataset cold storage, staged into HyperRAM

HyperBus is 8 bits DDR: bytes/s = 2 × fHB. Sustained efficiency after refresh collisions, row-boundary latency, and command overhead on long linear bursts is typically 70–85%. Verify the speed grade of the W957D8NB on your board against the Winbond datasheet before hard-committing a number — 166 vs 200 MHz is a 20% swing on every cyan number in §5. Also note the HyperBus clock you actually close in Agilex 3 GPIO is a timing-closure result, not a datasheet right; a conservative first pass at 100 MHz gives 200 MB/s peak.

Controller gap: Quartus does not ship a HyperBus controller, and the stock AXC3000 Nios V example runs from internal RAM only. Options, in order of least effort: (1) check the Arrow Agilex-3 GitHub reference designs for a released controller; (2) port an open-source portable controller (several proven VHDL/Verilog HyperBus masters exist and this bus is simple — CA phase, latency count, RWDS strobe); (3) licensed IP from the usual HyperBus vendors. Budget the port + timing closure as its own task, not a footnote.

Escape hatch for a future custom board: Agilex 3 EMIF supports LPDDR4 to 2133 Mbps — roughly a 10–15× lift on the memory wall. Everything memory-bound in §5 scales accordingly.

The design line. Below ~350–400 KB of INT8 weights (all four MLPerf Tiny models), the network lives entirely in M20K, FPGA AI Suite's DDR-free mode applies, and HyperRAM is left free to do exactly what you proposed: hold the test-record set. Above the line, HyperRAM becomes the weight store and the input path, the cyan column takes over, and net FPS drops two orders of magnitude into the 15–50 FPS class.

Traffic model for HyperRAM-resident weights: bytes/frame ≈ weights + input + ~2.5 MB inter-layer activation spill (rough constant; real spill depends on the architecture file's cache configuration — refine with the §9 estimator, which accepts memory bandwidth as an input). One more honesty clause: sequenced overlays carry fixed per-inference cost (descriptor setup, config, drain), so measured rates on sub-millisecond models land below the amber roofline. Quantifying that gap is a primary output of your benchmark.

§6 · The HyperRAM record-replay benchmark

Your method, engineered: records in, counters out

Store the quantized test set in HyperRAM with golden labels inline; a burst DMA replays records through a ping-pong buffer into the engine; a hardware scoreboard accumulates cycle counts and hit counts. FPS and accuracy fall out of two register reads — no host in the timed loop.

Record format

Pack offline with a Python script that applies the exact input quantization scale/zero-point from the compiled model, so hardware sees bit-identical tensors to the software reference:

Loop the resident set for sustained-rate runs; report cold-pass and looped numbers separately. For CIFAR-10 full-set accuracy, stage the remaining half from QSPI between passes — accuracy needs all 10,000, throughput does not.

Scoreboard register map (Avalon-MM, JTAG-visible)

AddrRegisterFunction
0x00CTRLstart · loop-enable · reset; self-clearing start
0x04N_RECORDSrecords per pass
0x08REC_STRIDE / BASErecord stride and HyperRAM base address
0x10CYCLES_64free-running counter latched first-issue → last-retire
0x18DONE_COUNTinferences retired
0x1CPASS_COUNTargmax == golden label
0x20LAT_MIN / LAT_MAXper-inference cycle extremes
0x28HIST[64]optional latency histogram, one M20K → p50/p99

Then: FPS = DONE_COUNT × f_clk / CYCLES_64 and accuracy = PASS_COUNT / DONE_COUNT. Two reads, no host jitter, defensible in a review.

Throughput and latency decouple the moment the engine overlaps in-flight requests — quote FPS from CYCLES_64 and the latency story from LAT_MIN/MAX and the histogram, never one as a proxy for the other.

§9 · Tool-flow path

Desk estimate → stock example → HyperRAM harness → custom RTL

PH 0

Desk-check every number in §5 — no hardware required

Install Quartus Prime Pro ≥25.3 (free Agilex 3 license; hard-IP use included with the device) and select FPGA AI Suite in the same installer. Run the performance estimator per candidate model and architecture file — since 25.1 it accepts external memory bandwidth as an input, added specifically for memory-limited parts like Agilex 3. Feed it your §5 slider value (~250 MB/s) and let the vendor tool arbitrate compute-vs-memory before you synthesize anything.

PH 1

Bring up the stock hostless example on the board

AI Suite 25.3 ships Agilex 3 production support including a hostless JTAG design for the Agilex 3 C-series dev kit — build it with dla_build_example_design.py, drive it from the streaming-capable System Console Python interface, and confirm the IP runs on your silicon before touching anything custom. Also relevant: the hostless DDR-free example ([HL-NO-DDR]) keeps the whole model in M20K with weights delivered as MIF files — that is the §5 "below the line" configuration, verbatim.

License gate: unlicensed IP generation carries a hard limit of 10,000 inference requests. Fine for accuracy passes on ≤10K records; for sustained-rate soak runs, generate licensed IP or plan a bitstream reload per run.

PH 2

Model preparation

TF/PyTorch → ONNX → OpenVINO IR (AI Suite 25.x tracks OpenVINO 2024.6) → NNCF post-training INT8 with a calibration slice → dla_compiler against your Agilex 3 architecture file. Record the compiled report's fold/streaming decisions — they set the real activation-spill term in the §5 traffic model. Keep an OpenVINO CPU-INT8 run of the same IR as the accuracy reference.

PH 3

HyperRAM integration

Select and port a HyperBus controller (§4 callout), close I/O timing at your target clock, and verify with an LFSR/address-in-data memtest + sustained-burst BW measurement before attaching inference — the measured MB/s replaces the cyan slider assumption. Platform Designer system: JTAG-Avalon master (or Nios V/g) + mSGDMA + HBMC + inference IP. Two build configs: (a) DDR-free IP, HyperRAM = record store only — the small-model sweet spot; (b) HyperRAM as IP global memory for >400 KB models, record store shrinks by the weight footprint.

PH 4

Benchmark harness

Python record packer (quantize with IR scales → append label → pad to 64 B → binary image, load via System Console master or QSPI). RTL scoreboard per the §6 register map. Host script pulls two registers, emits FPS/accuracy/p50/p99 per model, sweeps the model set.

PH 5

Cross-checks

Hardware accuracy must match the OpenVINO CPU-INT8 reference on identical records — a mismatch means layout or scale bugs, not "FPGA error." Power: inline USB-C meter for board watts per inference class; Quartus Power & Thermal Analyzer for the fabric-side estimate.

PH 6

Stretch: beat the overlay

Where the sequenced overlay's fixed cost dominates (KWS, anomaly detection), two escalations: the AI Suite Spatial Compiler (beta) maps MLP-class dataflow straight into fabric — vendor claims order-of-magnitude latency cuts at a fraction of the resources — and beyond that, custom dataflow RTL (hls4ml's Quartus/oneAPI path, or hand-rolled tensor-mode systolic) is the ceiling. Your method-A vs method-B data tells you exactly how much headroom that work buys.

§10 · Measurement checklist & risks

What a defensible result includes

  • Batch = 1, end-to-end from record fetch to scoreboard retire; report p50/p99 and FPS, cold and looped.
  • Accuracy on the full test set post-quantization, against the fp32 and CPU-INT8 baselines of the same graph.
  • Achieved HyperRAM MB/s from the memtest, quoted next to every memory-bound number.
  • fclk, resource utilization (ALM/DSP/M20K), and the architecture file logged per result — numbers without configs are noise.
  • Board watts via USB inline meter → µJ/inference; fabric estimate from the Power & Thermal Analyzer.
  • Fitter-report tensor-mode audit as a merge gate — DSPs left in classic 18×19 mode are a silent 10× loss (LV2 / L0).
  • Throughput and per-inference latency reported as separate numbers; pipelined engines decouple them.

Risk register

  • HyperBus timing closure at 166+ MHz in GPIO — mitigation: start at 100 MHz, measure, then push.
  • Refresh-collision jitter inflating p99 — mitigation: latch per-inference min/max; expect a bimodal histogram.
  • Depthwise layers (MobileNet family) mapping poorly to tensor mode — scheduler efficiency slider low end.
  • 10K-inference cap on unlicensed IP silently truncating soak runs — check DONE_COUNT against intent.
  • Quantization drift between packer and IR — mitigation: PH 5 parity gate is mandatory, not optional.
